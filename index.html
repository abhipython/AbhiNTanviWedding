Places to Visit in Vijayapura

Explore the rich history and culture of Vijayapura while you're here for the celebration.

Gol Gumbaz

The magnificent Gol Gumbaz is the mausoleum of Mohammed Adil Shah, featuring the world's second-largest dome. Its unique whispering gallery allows sounds to echo multiple times, creating an amazing acoustic experience. A must-visit architectural marvel of the Deccan.

Ibrahim Rauza

Often called the "Taj Mahal of the Deccan," Ibrahim Rauza is an exquisite example of Indo-Islamic architecture. This tomb and mosque complex is known for its elegant minarets, intricate carvings, and beautiful garden setting, showcasing the artistic excellence of the Adil Shahi dynasty.

Aihole Pattadakallu

These two are historically and architecturally significant sites, recognized as cradles of early Hindu temple architecture and declared UNESCO World Heritage sites for Pattadakal. These sites illustrate the evolution of Hindu temple architecture from the 5th to the 8th century.

Shivagiri

Shivagiri is a renowned pilgrimage and tourism site, famous for its colossal 85-foot-tall statue of Lord Shiva, depicting the deity in a meditative posture symbolizing peace and cosmic balance. — one of the largest Shiva statues in India. It is said to be the second tallest Shiva statue in Karnataka

Almatti

Beyond its engineering significance, Almatti has emerged as one of North Karnataka’s top tourist attractions, thanks to its landscaped surroundings and architectural gardens. Spanning 77 acres of recreational zone includes : Mughal, Rock, Japanese, Gopal Krishna, and Lavakush Gardens

Badami Cave Temples

Four stunning 6th–7th century rock-cut shrines carved into red sandstone cliffs, dedicated to Lord Shiva, Vishnu, and Jain Tirthankaras. The first cave showcases an 18-armed Nataraja in 81 dance poses, while others depict Vishnu’s avatars and intricate ceiling frescoes.
